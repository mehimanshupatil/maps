# Notes — Mumbai water supply (BMC City Development Plan 2005–2025, §13.1)

Source: `data/water supply mumbai bmc.pdf` (MCGM). Numbers are as of ~2005 unless noted;
daily figures in `data/history.json` supersede them. Curated for this project.

## Scheme history

| Scheme | Year | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Vihar | 1860 | First piped scheme; 3 earthen dams impounding the **Mithi river**; raised 1872 → 68 MLD |
| Tulsi | 1872–79 | Upstream of Vihar on the Mithi; 18 MLD via 600 mm main to Malabar Hill reservoir |
| Powai | 1891 | Emergency scheme on a Mithi tributary; inferior quality → dairy/agriculture only (defunct as supply) |
| Tansa | 1886–1948 | 4 stages; 2.8 km dam; water drawn from 7 outlet levels (375'–415' THD); ~410 MLD |
| Modak Sagar (Lower Vaitarna) | 1957 | "Vaitarna-cum-Tansa" scheme by N.V. Modak; **first pre-cooled-concrete dam in India**; 87 km main was then the world's longest pipeline; 7.2 km tunnel Vaitarna→Tansa lets surplus refill Tansa in dry years; +540 MLD |
| Upper Vaitarna | 1972 | GoM multipurpose: 2 dams (Alwandi + Vaitarna) ~80 km upstream of Modak Sagar; 60 MW hydro, then water released **down the river to Modak Sagar**; FSL 1980' MSL; system fully gravity-fed at this point |
| Bhatsa | 1981 / 1989 / 1997 (+2007) | Master plan by Binnie & Partners, World Bank aided; 3 stages × 455 MLD; pickup at **Pise weir**, treatment at **Panjrapur**; injected into Vaitarna mains at Yewai; MBR-II at Yewai (117 ML) |
| Middle Vaitarna | 2014 (proposed in doc) | RCC dam ~500 m long, ~100 m high between Upper/Lower Vaitarna; water via river to Modak Sagar, then 7.5 km tunnel (Belh Nallah) + 3000 mm main to Bhandup; +455 MLD |

## System facts

- Bhandup treatment works sited **on the shore of Vihar Lake** (why a separate "Vihar WTP" node is redundant). Backwash (~45 MLD) is released into Vihar Lake.
- Master Balancing Reservoirs: Bhandup 246 ML, Yewai 123 ML → **28 service reservoirs** → **110 supply zones**.
- ~4,000 km of mains (80–1800 mm); ~300 km primary + ~650 km secondary transmission.
- Intermittent supply: zones charged 2–6 h per 24 h (some 90 min); pressure 1–1.5 kg/cm².
- Notable tunnels: under Thane creek (Upper Vaitarna), Kasheli creek 3500 mm, Mahalaxmi→Malabar Hill 3000 mm (first TBM use in India), Bhandup→Malad→Charkop 12 km.
- Supply vs demand (doc's table): 2001: 3,025 vs 3,975 MLD (76%); projected 2021: 5,172 vs 5,068 (100%) assuming Gargai (455) and Pinjal (765) get built — neither has, as of 2026.
- 10–15% water cuts imposed in bad/delayed monsoon years.
